Post Impressionism

Using simplified colours and difinitive forms, post impressionism aimed to express emotions and themes instead of the optical impression that was the focus in the previous impressionism movement.

Famous Artist From the Movement

Vincent Van Gogh

1853-1890 Born in the Netherlands Van Gogh was one of the most iconcic artists of the post impressionism movement having made over 2000 pieces of art in his lifetime. Despite this he was not financially successful during his lifetime relying on his brother for finnancial aid, partly because many regarded him as a mardman due to his severe depression, pyscotic episodes and delusions whick eventually led to his suicide by shotgun.

Georges Seurat

1859-1891 Growing up in Paris Seurat was one of the leaders of the post impressionism movements devising techniques such as chromolunarism and pointilism. He had two children with artist model Madeleine Knobloch who's relationship with was concealed. The first was born in 1890 but died two weeks after Seurat did of an unknown disease that killed both the artist and his first son. His second child was born after his death but died during birth. Rather than focusing on fleeting moments and instead focus on the aspects of life that are unchanging such as urban leasuire which can be seen in his more prominent works 'Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grand Jatte'
